Conditions Library

Acne Vulgaris

Light skin tones: Erythema (redness), inflammatory papules, pustules.

Medium skin tones: Hyperpigmented papules, nodules, less visible redness.

Deep skin tones: Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, keloid risk, subtle inflammation.

Note: Pigment changes and scarring may be more prominent in darker skin tones.

Atopic Dermatitis (Eczema)

Light skin tones: Red, inflamed patches with scaling.

Medium skin tones: Purple or gray patches, lichenification.

Deep skin tones: Ashy, thickened, darkened skin with less visible redness.

Note: Erythema may be under-recognized in deeper skin tones.

Psoriasis

Light skin tones: Bright red plaques with silvery scale.

Medium skin tones: Salmon-colored plaques with gray scale.

Deep skin tones: Violet or dark brown plaques with minimal redness.

Note: Psoriasis is often underdiagnosed in patients with darker skin.
